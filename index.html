About the Builder

Miguiel Encinares, born December 8, 1998, quietly builds his work from Purok 4, Sitio Kawayanan, Brgy. Malico, Umingan Range, Nueva Ecija. A tech tinkerer who grew up loving anything that blinks, beeps, or breaks. He’s the type to debug code while the generator hums outside and the mountains fog up at dawn.

He’s got this strange hobyy of exhausting himself on purpose—coding sprints, night-long repair sessions, or taking on too many tasks—just to numb the noise of life. Instead of slowing down, he works faster. Instead of resting, he builds. It’s survival through productivity, and oddly, it works for him.

What KSL Is

A private off-grid workshop hidden in Umingan Range. No storefront. No logo outside. Just a small workspace powered by solar panels and stubbornness. Built for solving rural problems with practical tech.

Backstory

Miguiel didn’t plan to start a business. It just happened. Neighbors kept bringing broken phones. Then old routers. Then someone from the next barangay asked if he could build a rainfall logger. Eventually some local group asked if he could create a small CRM for tracking farm supply outputs. One project led to ten.

He built KSL unintentionally—like fixing one leak at a time until he accidentally renovated the whole house. Today, he keeps things low-key, building quietly for people who need someone who understands both tech and the realities of rural life.
